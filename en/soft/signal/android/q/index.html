Signal is the private and secure messaging app par excellence on Android. Discover all its tricks to get the most out of it with this guide

Signal app is a powerful communication tool focused on privacy and security. There are several reasons why we are convinced that this is the case. For example, end-to-end encryption is available for all transmissions, including voice and video calls. Moreover, behind it is a non-profit foundation, which does not aim to track you, and its code is open to all users.

All these virtues are in addition to a large number of functions available. It is capable of sending all types of files, from images to documents, lets the creation of groups, and even has a sticker selector, which they have named stickers. In short, Signal is private, secure, and full of useful features.

Signal is an instant messaging platform focused on privacy and security. The application, with the same name, acts as an official client and allows access to its services. Is Signal app legal?

Signal is a completely legal application. Its main purpose is to establish secure communications between two or more users. It fulfills this premise by letting you send text messages, images, videos, and other types of information. It also lets you make voice and video calls. All these transmissions are encrypted and can only be accessed by the people involved in a conversation or group.

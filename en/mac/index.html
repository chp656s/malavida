macOS

macOS is the name given to Apple’s operating system for laptops and desktop computers. Currently based on UNIX, it appeared in 1984 and was one of the first systems that included the integration of a mouse, a windows-based system, as well as icons and functional menus. The functionality of the classic Mac OS remained in force until its ninth version, in which it changed its name from Mac OS to OS X.
